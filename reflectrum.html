Reflectrum 2.0

Smart Mirror

Background
For Christmas in 2015 my boyfriend hacked together a smart mirror for me. I took his work and decided to redesign the UI.

Learn about how the hardware was crafted here.

Role
UI designer and front-end developer

Factors to Consider
Hardware
  • The screen that displayed content was smaller than the mirror itself. This means that if the background color of the screen was red, the mirror would look like a red screen with thick black edges.
  • Navigation through the mirror was done with a custom built controller.
Software
  • The software was built with web development tools (Electron, HTML, CSS, ReactJS).

Ideation 1.0


With my boyfriend and I being the primary (if not the only) users, validating features was relatively easy. Things we cared about was being able to frequently view time easily and occasionally check weather and calendar events.

We also thought about what happens if we’re away from the mirror for a certain amount of time and subsequently decided to include a screensaver page. After brainstorming what screensavers would be useful (and fun), we came up with a summary (includes time, date, and weather), Tetris (because I love Tetris), and clock.

Since I keep a booklet of inspirational quotes, I also thought it’d be neat to display them in the mirror.

After weeks of building, we had our first prototype:


Reflectrum 1.0 hung up on the wall!

Usability
After a week of using the mirror, I learned about how I could improve the user experience. Turns out I hardly used the inspirational quotes page. I also didn’t need an in depth page on weather since I used my phone’s weather app. But I knew I cared about knowing the current temperature and how the weather was currently like at a glance.

I also wanted less content filling the screen since I found it difficult to use the mirror for it’s original purpose.

Ideation 2.0
With the gained knowledge in mind, I decided to completely change the user flow. I wanted to simplify the entire experience. All that was needed was time, date, weather summary and fun screensavers.

Conclusion
Working on a project that combined hardware and web was a new experience for me. There were many new types of details to think about. Working on Reflectrum has helped me learn the value of user testing. As a designer I can assume but will learn the most the moment I conduct user research. Through usability tests is how I discover what people really want.

I am incredibly thankful for such a thoughtful boyfriend who spent countless hours working with me on the project. Reflectrum turned out to be a great learning experience together!
